Scaling a business can be learned

The best ideas and practices known to work distilled into a curriculum.

Have an idea

Ideas are many, actions are few. Pursue your idea, talk to people, refine, and repeat. Don’t write a business plan, but think of the problem you solve and for whom. Make it simple, initially really simple.

Build a team

The world has become flat and the people you need hide in a lot of places - find them. Trust your employees and minimize meetings and all other distractions. Productivity materializes magically when teams are aligned.

Tell your story

Replace marketing with education, outbound with inbound, broadcast with groupcast, and generate leads from fans. Forget about press releases and instead build your audience. Tell your story with video, images, sounds, and words.

Go-to-market

Start with GTM; you can fix everything else later. Who is your customer and how do you get to them? Make it narrow, really narrow. Don’t care about larger competitors but care about your customers.

Manage your board

Great investors are great mentors; hire them as you would employees. Understanding their metrics helps you adjust your actions. Manage expectations and you will stay in control of your company.

Know your metrics

Don’t guess your way to a plan with projected bookings, revenue, and expense. Nobody can do that. What you have to understand is your business model. Once you know and it is repeatable, you are ready to accelerate.
